Legislation Details

## Purpose and Intent

This Act aims to establish comprehensive data privacy protections for consumers while fostering innovation in the technology sector. The legislation builds upon existing privacy frameworks to create a unified approach to data protection nationwide.

## Key Provisions

### Section 1: Consumer Rights

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to delete personal information
  • Right to opt-out of the sale of personal information
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights
  • Right to data portability

    • ### Section 2: Business Obligations

    • Transparency requirements for data collection practices
    • Implementation of reasonable security measures
    • Data minimization principles
    • Purpose limitation for data use
    • Regular privacy impact assessments

    ### Section 3: Enforcement

    The legislation establishes a dedicated Data Protection Authority with the power to investigate violations, impose fines up to 4% of annual revenue, and mandate corrective actions. Private right of action is granted for certain violations with statutory damages ranging from $100 to $750 per consumer per incident.

    ### Section 4: Special Provisions for Sensitive Data

    Enhanced protections apply to sensitive personal information including health data, financial information, biometric data, and data concerning minors. Explicit consent is required for processing sensitive data, with additional safeguards for children under 16.

    ### Section 5: Cross-Border Data Transfers

    Organizations must ensure adequate protection for personal data transferred outside national borders through appropriate safeguards such as standard contractual clauses or binding corporate rules.

    ## Implementation Timeline

    • Q1 2025: Act passes into law
    • Q2 2025: Data Protection Authority established
    • Q3 2025: Guidelines and regulations published
    • Q1 2026: Full enforcement begins for large businesses (>$25M revenue)
    • Q3 2026: Full enforcement for all covered entities

    ## Economic Impact Assessment

    Independent analysis projects initial compliance costs of $2.8 billion across affected industries, with long-term benefits including increased consumer trust, reduced data breach costs, and harmonization of compliance requirements across jurisdictions. Small businesses with less than $5 million in annual revenue will have access to compliance assistance programs.
